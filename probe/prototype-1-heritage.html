About the Association

Founded in 1962, the District Court Bar Association represents the practising advocates of the district judiciary. For over six decades, the Association has stood for the independence of the Bar, the dignity of the profession, and access to justice for every citizen who enters the court complex.

The Association maintains the Bar library and reading rooms, conducts continuing legal education programmes, extends welfare support to member advocates and their families, and acts as the collective voice of the Bar before the Bench, the Bar Council, and the Government.

Membership is open to every advocate enrolled with the State Bar Council who ordinarily practises before the courts of this district. The affairs of the Association are managed by an Executive Body elected annually by the General Body.
