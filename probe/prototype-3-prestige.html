About the Association

Since 1962, the District Bar Association has been the collective conscience of the district Bar — defending the independence of advocates and the rights of those they represent.

Our Members

Over 1,240 advocates enrolled with the State Bar Council and practising before the courts of the district form the General Body of the Association.

Our Work

The Association maintains the Bar library, conducts continuing legal education, administers a member welfare fund, and organises legal aid camps.

Our Governance

An Executive Body — President, Secretary and elected members — is chosen each year by the General Body to manage the Association's affairs.
