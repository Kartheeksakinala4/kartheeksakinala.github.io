Data Recovery Services

Failed drive, corrupted volume, or locked-out encryption — if the data physically exists, we have the lab, the tools, and the expertise to get it back.

Damaged Media Recovery

Recovery at every level of failure

Media fails in different ways, and each failure demands a different discipline. We diagnose first, then recover at the level your media actually needs.

Logical Recovery

The drive is healthy but the data isn't reachable — deleted files, formatted partitions, corrupted file systems, ransomware aftermath, or OS failures. We rebuild structures and carve data without ever writing to your original media.

  • Deleted & formatted data recovery
  • File system and partition repair
  • Corrupted database and file reconstruction
Learn more

Firmware-Level Recovery

When a drive's own microcode fails — corrupted service areas, translator faults, or SSD controller issues — the data is intact but invisible. Using specialised hardware tools, we repair firmware and regain access safely.

  • HDD service-area and translator repair
  • SSD controller and NAND-level work
  • Vendor-specific firmware fault handling
Learn more

Physical Recovery

Clicking drives, head crashes, motor seizures, water and fire damage. In a controlled cleanroom environment we transplant heads, swap platters, and image failing media sector by sector before it degrades further.

  • Head-stack and spindle motor replacement
  • Water, fire, and impact-damaged media
  • Cleanroom imaging of degrading drives
Learn more
Encrypted Media

BitLocker Recovery

Encryption protects your data — until it locks you out of it. A failed drive, a lost recovery key, corrupted metadata, or a TPM change can leave a BitLocker volume inaccessible even though the data inside is intact.

  • Locked & corrupted BitLocker volumes

    Recovery from damaged BitLocker metadata and inaccessible encrypted partitions.

  • Recovery-key based unlocking

    Decryption using available recovery keys, escrowed credentials, or domain/Azure AD key retrieval.

  • Failing encrypted drives

    Imaging a dying BitLocker drive first, then decrypting the image — so the failure never costs you the data.

Good to Know

What to do when data goes missing

01

Power it down

Stop using the device immediately. Every write — even booting — can overwrite recoverable data.

02

Don't DIY a failing drive

Recovery software on a physically failing drive can cause permanent damage. Clicking sounds mean stop.

03

Note what happened

What was deleted, when it failed, any error messages — details speed up diagnosis considerably.

04

Call us

Free diagnosis and a clear quote before any recovery work begins. No data, no fee on most cases.
